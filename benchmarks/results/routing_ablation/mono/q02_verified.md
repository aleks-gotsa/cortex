# Executive Summary

This research synthesizes key differences between GPT-4 and Claude, focusing on their performance metrics and features. Both models are advanced language models developed by leading AI companies—OpenAI for GPT-4 and Anthropic for Claude. While both have demonstrated significant improvements over their predecessors, they differ in terms of context window size, multimodal capabilities, and ethical considerations.

GPT-4 has a larger context window (up to 32K tokens) compared to Claude's default 8K context window, allowing it to handle more complex tasks with greater detail. GPT-4 also supports multimodal inputs, enabling interaction through images as well as text. In terms of performance metrics, GPT-4 has shown improvements in coding and security scenarios, though it still faces challenges such as producing code vulnerable to SQL injection attacks.

Claude, on the other hand, is known for its ethical alignment techniques, including 'constitutional AI,' which aims to enhance compliance with ethical standards. Claude's capabilities include advanced features like web search integration and computer use simulation, making it versatile in various applications. However, its release has faced legal challenges due to concerns over mass domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons.

Both models have seen updates and enhancements since their initial releases, indicating ongoing development efforts by their respective companies. The key differences lie in their context handling, multimodal support, and ethical considerations, which impact their suitability for different use cases.
