## Major Open-Source LLMs Released by 2026
By 2026, several major open-source LLMs have been released:
1. **Qwen3** from QwenLM: This model series includes various sizes such as 8 billion, 30 billion, and 250 billion parameters. It supports multiple inference frameworks including SGLang, vLLM, and TensorRT-LLM.
2. **DeepSeek-V3**: Developed by DeepSeek-AI, this model is designed for high-performance inference on various hardware platforms, supporting BF16 and FP8 modes.

## Key Features and Capabilities of Open-Source LLMs
Open-source LLMs like Qwen3 and DeepSeek-V3 are equipped with several advanced features:
1. **Multi-Step Reasoning**: Both models support multi-step reasoning tasks, enabling them to handle complex instructions and perform multiple steps of logical deduction.
2. **Ultra-Long Input Handling**: Qwen3 supports ultra-long inputs up to 1 million tokens, making it suitable for handling extensive context in natural language processing tasks.
3. **Compatibility with Multiple Inference Frameworks**: These models are compatible with various inference frameworks such as SGLang, vLLM, and TensorRT-LLM, allowing users to deploy them on different hardware platforms.

## Performance Metrics of Open-Source LLMs
The performance metrics of open-source LLMs in 2026 show competitive results across various benchmarks:
1. **IFEval**: Qwen3 performs well on IFEval tasks, demonstrating strict adherence to formatting instructions.
2. **BBH (Big Bench Hard)**: DeepSeek-V3 shows strong performance on BBH subtasks, indicating its capability to handle complex reasoning and knowledge-based tasks.
